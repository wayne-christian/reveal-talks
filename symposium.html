Invisible Worlds

Augmented reality story telling app for the National Trust in Alderley Edge

Collaborative

Invisible Worlds is a collaboration between an interdisciplinary team of researchers at the University of Birmingham, the Institute of Historical Research at the University of London, and the University of Lincoln, alongside cultural partners (the Blackden Trust, the Derbyshire Caving Club, and the National Trust)

Alderley Edge is associated with a romantic local Legend of Alderley Edge which tells of the wizard Merlin and a band of sleeping warriors who will rise and save our country in its hour of need.

We developed an innovative geo-located audio and augmented reality tour that encourages visitors to share and view stories inspired by Alderley Edge.
Visitors could view geo-located content at the edge or immerse themselves remotely via an ancient portal that transported the user to key locations using 360 video footage to inspire their own narratives.

Collaboration with Local schools and communities

With support from the Caving club, mines were three dimensionally scanned to allow users to walk in them in AR

We developed a geo-localised map and digitised trails for the park whilst curating soundscapes and AR experiences in key but safe areas. The next video demonstrates the process utilising Google Earth Pro
